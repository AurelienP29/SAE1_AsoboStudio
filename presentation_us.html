Who is Asobo Studio ?


Asobo Studio is a video game developer based in Bordeaux, France, renowned for its creative approach and technical expertise. Founded in 2002, the studio has made a name for itself on the international scene with a variety of titles that combine innovation and immersive storytelling. It is particularly renowned for its critically acclaimed games, such as A Plague Tale: Innocence and A Plague Tale: Requiem, which explore dark and moving themes through captivating gameplay mechanics. In addition, Asobo has redefined flight simulation with Microsoft Flight Simulator (2020), offering a realistic and detailed experience that has seduced enthusiasts of the genre. With its talented team, Asobo Studio continues to explore new ideas and push the boundaries of video games, consolidating its reputation as a major player in the industry.

Asobo history
Founded in Bordeaux in 2002, Asobo Studio began its career as an independent video game developer, specializing in the creation of titles for a variety of platforms. Over the years, the studio made a name for itself by collaborating with major companies, notably developing games for iconic franchises such as Pixar and Microsoft. Their first big success came with Fuel in 2009, which demonstrated their ability to create vast, immersive open worlds. However, it was with 2019's A Plague Tale: Innocence that the studio really exploded onto the international scene, winning numerous awards for its poignant narrative and dark atmosphere. The success of this title led to the production of its sequel, A Plague Tale: Requiem, in 2022. At the same time, Asobo was commissioned to revive the popular flight simulation franchise with Microsoft Flight Simulator in 2020, a project that received rave reviews for its realism and accuracy. Today, Asobo Studio is recognized as a major player in the video game industry, combining technological innovation with captivating storytelling.

Asobo Studio's Technological Innovation and its Impact on the Video Game Industry
Asobo Studio stands out for its innovative use of technology and development techniques, enabling it to make a significant impact on the video game industry. The studio has integrated advanced motion capture and 3D modeling tools to create immersive environments and realistic characters, as seen in A Plague Tale: Innocence and A Plague Tale: Requiem. What's more, their work on Microsoft Flight Simulator has redefined flight simulation standards, using satellite mapping data and state-of-the-art graphics rendering to deliver a gaming experience of unparalleled precision. This technical approach has inspired other developers to adopt similar methods, fostering innovation in game design. By integrating interactive storytelling with fluid game mechanics, Asobo has influenced the way stories are told in video games, setting new standards that are pushing the industry towards ever-expanding creative horizons.

Asobo Studio was founded in 2002 by a passionate team of video game professionals, including Hugues Ricour, Philippe “Pip” Thibaut, Jean-Baptiste Mazzoleni and Romain Pichon. As Managing Director, Hugues Ricour played a key role in the studio's strategic direction and the development of innovative projects. Philippe Thibaut contributed his expertise in game design and art direction, helping to shape the visual identity of the studio's titles. Jean-Baptiste Mazzoleni and Romain Pichon, with their technical skills, helped lay the solid foundations on which the studio has flourished. Together, these founding members have created a collaborative environment that fosters creativity and innovation, propelling Asobo Studio to success and enabling it to make games that captivate gamers around the world.

Asobo Studio's popular games
Asobo Studio is a French video game developer renowned for its creativity and production quality. Among its best-known titles, A Plague Tale: Innocence (2019) and its sequel A Plague Tale: Requiem (2022) stand out for their immersive storytelling, following the poignant tale of Amicia and her brother Hugo, who struggle to survive in plague-ravaged medieval France. In contrast, Microsoft Flight Simulator (2020) offers an unprecedented flight simulation experience, allowing players to pilot aircraft through a beautifully recreated world, using satellite mapping data. In addition to these successes, Asobo has also contributed to The Crew 2 (2018), an open-world racing game, although it is not the lead developer. Their first title, Fuel (2009), also made its mark with its vast open world, offering players a multitude of racing challenges. Through these games, Asobo Studio demonstrates impressive versatility and a commitment to captivating gaming experiences.
